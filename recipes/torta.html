Margherita Cake

Simplicity and Tasty

Margherita Cake is a classic Italian dessert, soft and delicate, decorated with powdered sugar.
In the past, Margherita Cake was considered a dessert for special occasions, prepared mainly during the Christmas
and Easter holidays. It was often given as a gift to friends and relatives as a sign of affection and hospitality.
Its peasant origins are evident in the simplicity of the ingredients and the preparation, which did not require the use
of particularly sophisticated tools or techniques.

The name: a tribute to nature
The name "Margherita" means "Daisy" and comes from the similarity that the cake takes on when cut into slices.
The layer of powdered sugar that covers the surface resembles the white petals of the flower,
while the yellow batter recalls the golden center.

Ingredients

  • 100g granulated sugar (or powdered sugar)
  • 2 eggs
  • 80ml vegetable oil
  • 200ml cow's milk or plant-based milk
  • 200g cake flour
  • 1 sachet of baking powder
  • Optional: Lemon/Vanilla/Vanilla extract
  • Decoration: powdered sugar

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 180°C, with the base on the center shelf.
  2. Combine the eggs and sugar, beating well until the mixture is frothy.
  3. Gradually add the oil and mix, then incorporate the milk.
  4. Add all the dry ingredients (flour and baking powder) and mix well, eliminating any lumps.
  5. Grease and flour a cake pan of at least 20-18 cm in diameter.
  6. Pour the batter into the cake pan and bake at 180°C for 30 minutes.
  7. Check for doneness with a toothpick.
  8. Once cooled, sprinkle with plenty of powdered sugar.
  9. Tips: The cake can be paired with: Jams, Dried fruit...great for any time of day!

Margherita Cake stays soft for several days, it is advisable to keep it in a cake stand or an airtight container
